# PRODUCT.md — Jarvis Desktop Companion

## Product purpose
A personal AI butler for one user (Alex, Dublin). Tray-resident Electron app: morning briefing,
job-search tracking, finance coaching, voice in/out, and a summonable full-screen HUD. The HUD
("the Summon", Ctrl+Shift+J) is the hero surface: it must feel like Tony Stark's JARVIS, not a
web dashboard.

## Users
Exactly one: the owner. Power user, CS graduate, judges UI hard ("anything short of the Iron-Man
bar gets re-forged"). Uses it many times a day in short bursts: summon, glance, command, dismiss.
Ambient conditions: a laptop, any hour, often a dim room in the evening.

## Brand & tone
- Cinematic sci-fi HUD: precision instrument, not decoration. Everything looks *measured*.
- Butler personality: dry, understated, British. The UI equivalent: restraint, fine hairlines,
  quiet confidence. Never loud, never busy.
- Signature elements: arc-reactor orb, holographic cyan light on a dimmed room, radial/segmented
  instrument details, uppercase micro-labels with wide tracking.

## Anti-references (never look like)
- SaaS admin dashboards (cards, shadows, rounded widgets).
- Cyberpunk neon overload (magenta/green glow soup, glitch effects).
- Generic "AI assistant" chat bubbles.

## Strategic principles
- Glanceable in under 3 seconds: focus items, time, money, jobs. Hierarchy is sacred.
- 60fps or it doesn't ship (transform/opacity animation only; runs on a modest laptop).
- Dark by physics, not by fashion: it is a light overlay projected onto a dimmed desktop.
- Every pixel diegetic: elements read as parts of one instrument, not widgets on a page.
